\begin{frame}{Motivation}
  \hypertarget{starting_page}{}

  Reproducibility is now widely recognized as a central concern in empirical research. Recent work  emphasizes that many irreproducible results stem not from flawed methods, but from undocumented defaults, missing metadata, and fragile workflows \cite{lambda2024defaults}. Here, we argue that even \emph{entirely plausible} results may \emph{fail to reproduce} when computational environments are not explicitly specified.
